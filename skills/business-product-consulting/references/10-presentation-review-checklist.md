# Presentation Review Checklist

Related: [SCR/SCQA](02-scr-scqa-communication.md), [pyramid principle](03-pyramid-principle.md), [MECE issue trees](04-mece-issue-trees.md), [risk](09-risk-and-second-order-effects.md)

## Purpose

Use this as a pre-flight review for a memo, deck, RFC, demo narrative, incident report or leadership update.

## Core Test

The audience should understand in the first minute:

- what is happening;
- why it matters;
- what you recommend;
- why the recommendation is credible;
- what decision or action is needed.

## Executive Storyline

```text
Recommendation:
Situation:
Complication:
Decision question:
Reasons:
Evidence:
Risks:
Ask:
```

## Slide Or Memo Checklist

- The first page contains the main answer.
- Situation is short and shared.
- Complication creates urgency.
- Recommendation is concrete.
- Supporting points are logically grouped.
- Each chart or data point has a `so what`.
- Alternatives and trade-offs are visible.
- Risks are specific.
- Next decision is explicit.
- The narrative is tailored to the audience.

## Evidence Checklist

- Evidence supports the claim above it.
- Metrics are connected to user or business outcome.
- Activity metrics are not treated as impact.
- Data caveats are stated.
- Qualitative evidence is not overgeneralized.
- Counterevidence is acknowledged.

## Design And UI/UX Review Checklist

- Does the screen support the main user decision or job?
- Are primary actions obvious?
- Are edge states, error states and recovery paths covered?
- Does the UI reduce cognitive load?
- Does it expose status and confidence where needed?
- Does it avoid creating support burden?
- Are accessibility and instrumentation considered?
- Does visual polish serve the outcome?

## Deck Anti-Patterns

- Status-report deck: pages summarize work but never argue.
- Data dump: charts without implications.
- Roadmap theater: features without trade-offs or outcomes.
- Hidden ask: audience does not know what to decide.
- Technical-first narrative for business audience.
- Business buzzwords without operational detail.
- Risks listed as vague "adoption risk" or "technical risk" only.

## Example Prompts

- "Review this deck for answer-first storyline and missing ask."
- "Convert this technical update into an executive narrative."
- "Add so-what titles to these slides."
- "Find risks, trade-offs and unsupported claims in this memo."
